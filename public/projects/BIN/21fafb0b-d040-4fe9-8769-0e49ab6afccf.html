About the Jovian System

Orbital Resonance

The three inner Galilean moons (Io, Europa, Ganymede) are locked in a special 1:2:4 orbital resonance, known as the Laplace resonance. For every orbit Ganymede completes, Europa completes exactly two orbits, and Io completes exactly four orbits. This gravitational interaction maintains their orbital eccentricities and influences geological activity, particularly on Io. Callisto, the outermost Galilean moon, does not participate in this resonance.

Io

The most volcanically active body in the Solar System. Its activity is driven by tidal heating caused by Jupiter's gravity and the orbital resonance with Europa and Ganymede. Orbital Period: ~1.77 Earth days.

Europa

Features a smooth, icy crust believed to cover a vast subsurface saltwater ocean, making it a prime candidate for extraterrestrial life. Orbital Period: ~3.55 Earth days.

Ganymede

The largest moon in the Solar System (larger than Mercury). It's the only moon known to have its own magnetic field. Features both dark, cratered regions and lighter, grooved terrain. Orbital Period: ~7.15 Earth days.

Callisto

Heavily cratered and ancient surface, indicating minimal geological activity. It orbits outside the main resonance. Orbital Period: ~16.69 Earth days.
