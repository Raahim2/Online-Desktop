What are Chladni Figures?

Chladni figures, named after German physicist Ernst Chladni, are patterns that emerge when a flat surface (like a metal plate) covered with a thin layer of particles (like sand or salt) is vibrated at a resonant frequency.

The vibrations cause the plate to oscillate. The particles are bounced away from areas of high vibration (antinodes) and collect along the lines where the plate is relatively still (nodal lines). These nodal lines form intricate geometric patterns that depend on the frequency of vibration, the shape of the plate, and how it's supported.

This simulator uses placeholder images to represent some common patterns typically identified by mode numbers (m, n), which relate to the number of nodal lines crossing the plate horizontally and vertically.
